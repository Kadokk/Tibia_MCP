# CipSoft Fansite-Programme Inquiry

**Status:** draft — awaiting owner send. Do not treat as sent until the checklist below is filled in.

## Submission mechanism (verified live 2026-07-21)

Source: `https://www.tibia.com/community/?subtopic=fansites&page=programme` (linked from
`https://www.tibia.com/community/?subtopic=fansites`).

The page states: *"If you have created a Tibia fansite and are interested in taking part in
our fansite programme, please send an email to [the programme address] with a short
description of your fansite's content."* The address is a `tibia.com` mailbox. There is no
web form — email is the sole submission channel. There is also a separate "Fansite
Agreement" document governing already-accepted fansites (monetization rule: *"all means of
monetising your fansite must be made clear to visitors"*; ads/sponsors allowed if suitable
for a minors-inclusive audience).

**Owner action required before sending:** copy the exact email address directly from the
live page above at send-time — this doc intentionally does not hand-transcribe it, to avoid
a stale or mistyped address going out.

## Draft email body (≤400 words)

> Subject: Fansite Programme Inquiry — TibiaEdge (AI Discord Assistant)
>
> Hello CipSoft team,
>
> My name is [OWNER NAME]. I'm writing to introduce TibiaEdge and ask a question about the
> fansite programme's monetization rules before we launch publicly.
>
> **What TibiaEdge is:** a Discord bot that acts as an AI assistant for Tibia players. Users
> ask natural-language questions — about items, creatures, spells, NPCs, hunting spots, and
> quests — and the bot answers using a structured knowledge base built from TibiaWiki content
> (imported under its CC BY-SA license, with attribution and a source link on every answer)
> plus public, unauthenticated Tibia.com pages (e.g. highscores, character/auction lookups a
> browser can already see). TibiaEdge does not interact with the game client, does not
> automate gameplay or botting, and does not read or emulate the game's network protocol.
> It only reads public web data and answers questions in Discord.
>
> **Monetization:** we plan a freemium model — a free tier covering core lookups, and an
> optional premium tier (around $4.99/month) that adds features like long-running quest
> tracking and personalized reminders. No feature we're planning touches gameplay itself;
> it's assistant convenience on top of publicly available information.
>
> **Our question:** given the fansite agreement's requirement that "all means of monetising
> your fansite must be made clear to visitors," we want to confirm — before we take any
> payment — whether a paid subscription tier for AI-assisted, wiki- and public-data-grounded
> features like this is acceptable under the fansite programme, or whether CipSoft has
> concerns/conditions we should design around first.
>
> We'd rather ask now than build something we'd have to unwind later. Happy to share more
> detail (screenshots, a feature list, or a test invite) if that's useful for your review.
>
> Thank you for your time,
> [OWNER NAME]
> [CONTACT EMAIL / DISCORD]
